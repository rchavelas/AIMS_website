Smart Manufacturing

Smart manufacturing, automation & materials.

Convergence of advanced processes, robotics and materials innovation. The emphasis is on connecting materials design, process optimization and system-level performance — enabling next-generation production capabilities.

  • Sustainable and energy-efficient manufacturing
  • Advanced and high-performance materials
  • Collaborative robotics and flexible cells
  • Multi-objective process optimization
  • Additive and hybrid manufacturing
  • Vision-based inspection and quality systems

Industrial AI

Artificial intelligence for industrial systems.

AI doesn't operate in isolation. Its effectiveness depends on integration with sensing systems, computational infrastructure and physical layers — control, robotics, energy platforms. AIMS moves from isolated models to fully integrated industrial applications.

  • Machine learning and production inference
  • Plant and line digital twins
  • Autonomous and adaptive decision-making
  • Computer vision and distributed sensing
  • Energy and operations optimization
  • Trustworthy AI and observability systems

Supply Chain

Supply chain & logistics systems.

End-to-end supply chain design, optimization and transformation. We integrate AI, optimization methods and empirical data to address resilience, efficiency and sustainability — with particular attention to Scope 3, regulatory pressure and global volatility.

  • Digital transformation of supply chains
  • Smart logistics and last-mile delivery
  • Distribution network design strategies
  • Resilient and disruption-resistant chains
  • Warehouses of the future
  • Scope 3 visibility and traceability

LIFT Lab ● IN COLLABORATION WITH MIT CTL

Low-Income Firms Transformation Lab.

Applying AI to the challenge of micro and small enterprises, particularly in low-data environments. Built at MIT CTL and actively expanding at Tec de Monterrey, it combines technological innovation with large-scale field deployment — measurable productivity, inclusion and resilience.

  • AI with scarce and imperfect data
  • AI avatars and tutors for training
  • Image recognition for inventory
  • AI assistants for SMEs
  • Integration with inclusive supply chains
  • Public policy and social impact

Research model

Impact research,
not isolated discovery.

AIMS doesn't replace disciplinary capabilities. It integrates and amplifies them — and orients them toward deployable outcomes, not isolated publications.

01 / Identify

A real industrial challenge

We work from the industrial partner's challenge: a problem with a defined impact metric, not an abstract academic topic.

02 / Integrate

Multi-disciplinary by design

We cross areas and campuses from day one — manufacturing with AI, supply chain with materials — because real problems don't respect disciplines.

03 / Deploy

Pilots in plant and field

Deployment is part of the work, not a later step. We validate under real conditions with real data.
